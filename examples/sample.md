# The Lighthouse at Dawn

The old lighthouse had stood on the cliff for over a century. Every morning,
the keeper climbed its spiral stairs to watch the sun rise over the water,
and every evening he lit the lamp that guided ships safely past the rocks.

<mark>Visitors often asked him whether the job ever grew lonely. He would smile and
say that the sea was company en</mark>ough, with its changing moods and
endless conversation of waves against the shore.

## <mark>A Note on the Lamp</mark>

<mark>The lamp itself was a marvel of simple engineering. A single wick, kept
trimmed and clean, could throw light for nearly twenty miles on a clear
night. The keeper refilled the oil reservoir each afternoon so the
flame would never falter in the dark.</mark>

When storms rolled in, the routine changed. The keeper would stay awake
through the night, checking the lamp and listening to the wind. Sailors said
that as lon<mark>g as the light still turned, they knew exactly where the danger
lay and how to steer around it.</mark>

## <mark>Why It Still Matters</mark>

<mark>Long after automation replaced the keeper, people kept visiting the</mark> cliff.
They came for the view, for the quiet, and for the reminder that
a small, steady light can matter more than a brilliant one that
flickers.
